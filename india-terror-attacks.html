Terror attacks in India: frequency and religion / ideology share

This page tracks terror attacks in India with at least 1 death on a 1947–2026 axis. Because the sourced event data used here start later, the chart spans the full timeline but only plots attacks where defensible incident-level data are available. The first graph shows total attacks per year; the second shows the yearly religion / ideology share of attributed perpetrator groups.

Method

What is counted and how the share is classified

I interpret your request literally as attacks with 1 or more deaths. The source used here is a public GTD-derived India incident dataset mirror covering attacks through 2016. Religion share is classified conservatively by the named perpetrator group’s religion or broad ideology where that is reasonably attributable from the group label; ambiguous or weakly attributable cases stay in Unknown / other.

Timeline span vs data span

The axis runs from 1947 to 2026 for historical framing, but the plotted attack data used here begin in 1979 and end in 2016 in the public event dataset mirror I could verify directly in this environment.[1][2]

Why “religion share” is approximate

This is not a claim about the personal religion of every attacker. It is a classification of the attributed perpetrator group into broad religion / ideology buckets such as Islamist, Sikh separatist, Hindu extremist, left-wing / Maoist, ethno-nationalist / separatist, and unknown / other.[3]

Source notes

  1. Public GitHub mirror of a GTD-derived CSV used here — the India rows in this mirror run through 2016; attack counts on this page are filtered to India and 1+ deaths.
  2. Global Terrorism Database (START / University of Maryland) — official GTD project page; used as the underlying event-database reference for the public mirror cited above.
  3. Religion / ideology shares on this page are not direct measures of the personal religion of every attacker. They are conservative categorizations of the attributed perpetrator group labels visible in the event data, with ambiguous cases left in Unknown / other.
