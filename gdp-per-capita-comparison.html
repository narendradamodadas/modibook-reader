GDP per capita: India compared with Pakistan, China and Bangladesh

This page compares long-run GDP per capita on a 1947–2026 axis. The timeline starts at independence for context, but the Maddison-based annual series used here begin in 1950. A vertical marker shows when Narendra Modi became prime minister in 2014.

Method

Maddison historical GDP per capita series

I use the Our World in Data grapher series for Maddison-style historical GDP per capita because it extends much further back than World Bank API series and allows a post-independence comparison across India, Pakistan, China and Bangladesh.

Timeline span

The x-axis runs from 1947 to 2026 for readability and political context. The underlying annual GDP per capita data used here begin in 1950 and currently run through 2022 in the sourced series.[1]

Modi marker

A vertical marker at 2014 shows the start of Narendra Modi’s tenure as prime minister. It is a visual historical marker only; it does not itself imply causation for everything that happens after 2014.[2]
